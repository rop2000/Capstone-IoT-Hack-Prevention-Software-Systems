    Computer Science Capstone

    My Capstone

    How can the security of Internet of Things (IoT) devices be improved to provide safer consumer products?

    About Me

    I am a Senior at Farmington High School in Farmington, CT. I am currently enrolled in the Custom Capstone course where I will be focusing on Computer Science. I'm interested in all kinds of programming languages and my main interests are in web design as well as Java programming. I am very passionate about this field and hope to be involved with cybersecurity and data analysis in the future.

    Capstone Goals

    IoT Systems

    My primary goal is to understand how internet connected devices work, physically and wirselessly, through research and colloboration with other experts working in this field.

    Custom Device

    After I finish researching the basics, I will further research how to create a custom device that can hack into IoT products and the steps that can be taken to prevent such a hack from occurring. I will also develop software that can be installed to prevent a hack.

    Community Outreach

    Once I have a final device designed and working, I will present my research and product to local companies or community contacts for further feedback. I also hope that other groups can use this research to improve or test their own products.

    Monthly Reflections

    September

    This month I came up with several topics that I am interested in reaserching, with input from my Capstone Advisor, Mr.Marshall, and my Capstone Interview. I have narrowed my topic down to consumer safety in regards to Internet of Thigns devices. I will further develop this idea over the next month to come up with a driving question .

    October

    This month was spent researching how IoT devices work, what kind of signals and encryption are used, and how I could demonstrate this problem and the steps taken to prevent a hack from occurring.

    November

    During the first week, I researched the different devices that I could test my proposed device on. This was narrowed down to iDevices Security system and Amazonâ€™s Alexa home speaker. During the second week, my advisor recommended contacting a software division representative to gain access to further resources or a community contact. I emailed Paul Langdon, a software engineer at the company for further help. By the end of November, I had a final plan for engineering my device and set a goal to finish the majority of my software by January.

    December

    After finishing all of the research I needed, I contacted a student at CalTech for more help on creating my device. He recommended using an Arduino, a customizable computer chip, for programming and creating my project. As a result, I ordered several parts and items to start building it over the Holiday Break. Some problems I encountered were having incorrect parts/broken parts, which delayed me. The part that connected my device to the internet was obstructing the way for other needed parts, which took an additional 3 days to rewire and reprogram. However, at the end of break, I had a semi-completed device that could clone unlocking signals and access basic IoT devices.

    January

    After finishing building the majority of my device, I am moving forward with the software side of my project. In order to test my hack prevention program, I will be ordering a consumer device for a test-subject. I have also contacted a researcher at the California Institute of Technology who is helping me with making this program compatible with multitudes of devices.

    Timeline

    This a timeline of what I am planning to complete over the next two months.

    • Making My Arduino-Based Device Internet Compatible

      In order to demonstrate my hack prevention software live during my final presentation, I need everything to be internet-connected. Over the next two weeks, I will be developing a website that displays data coming to my device and allow me to control it with a click of a button. This aspect of my Capstone is essential because it will help me explain my project in laymans terms and provide a visual aid of what occurs behind the scenes.

    • Purchasing an IoT Smart Plug

      In order to provide proof-of-concept during my final presentation, I need a real consumer product that my software can tested on. I am currently researching the different types of smart plugs avaiable on the market; specifically, ones that I can work with.

    • Testing & Modifying Code

      After I have purchased a smart plug that meets my needs, I will continue testing and modifying my code so that my device will be able to hack into it, and then prevent the hack during a second round.

    • Final Product & Presentation

      Once my product is fully developed, I will 3-D print a case or stylized box to make it user friendly. I will also do several practice runs of my presentation to ensure that I can handle any situation that may come my way during the real one (Ex. slow device, website not loading). Alongside this practice, I will be contacting the Network Administrators in the high school to make sure my device is not blocked on the school WiFi.
